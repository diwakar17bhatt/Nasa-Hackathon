25143 Vesta Asteroid


Discovery

One of the largest and earliest known asteroids,Vesta was discovered on March 29th,1807,and was visited by the Dawn mission in 2011.

Size

The asteroid Vesta has a mean diameter of around 330 miles (530 kilometers). It's the third largest asteroid in the asteroid belt, after Ceres and Pallas, but it's the second most massive object in the belt

Distace from Earth

Vesta is located approximately 100 million miles from Earth in the solar system's main asteroid belt—home to countless bodies that circle the sun between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter.

Velocity of Asteroid

The average orbital speed of the asteroid Vesta is 19.34 kilometers per second.

Period of Rotation

The asteroid Vesta's rotation period is 5.34 hours, which is relatively fast for an asteroid. Vesta's rotation is also prograde, meaning its north pole points in the direction of right ascension 20 hours and 32 minutes, declination +48°.

Orbital Period

The orbital period of the asteroid Vesta is 3.63 years, or 1,325 days
