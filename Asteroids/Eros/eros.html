433 Eros Asteroid


Discovery

Eros was discovered on Aug. 13, 1898, by Gustav Witt, director of the Urania Observatory in Berlin, and independently observed on the same date by Auguste H.P. Charlois in Nice, France. In a break with tradition at the time, the asteroid was given a male name: Eros, the Greek god of love and son of Mercury and Venus.

Size

Asteroid 433 Eros is approximately 33 by 13 by 13 kilometers (20.5 by 8 by 8 miles) in size, making it one of the larger near-Earth asteroids. It's also one of the most elongated asteroids, with a potato-like shape.

Distace from Earth

The distance of Asteroid 433 Eros from Earth is currently 350,800,091 kilometers, equivalent to 2.344954 Astronomical Units.

Velocity of Asteroid

The average orbital speed of the asteroid 433 Eros is 24.36 kilometers per second.

Period of Rotation

The asteroid 433 Eros rotates on its axis every 5.27 hours. The rotation rate of Eros is 1639.38922±0.00015°/day. Eros was the first asteroid to be discovered to have variations in brightness due to its rotation. These periodic light fluctuations were used to determine its rotation period.

Orbital Period

The orbital period of the asteroid 433 Eros is 1.76 years, or 643 Earth days. Eros is a near-Earth asteroid that orbits the Sun in a slightly elliptical trajectory. Its orbit crosses Mars' path, but does not intersect Earth's.
