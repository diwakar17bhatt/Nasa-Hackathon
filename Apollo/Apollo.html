Discovery

The group is named after 1862 Apollo, which was discovered in 1932 by German astronomer Karl Reinmuth. It was lost shortly after, but rediscovered in 1978.

characteristics

Apollo asteroids have orbits that are larger than Earth's semi-major axis, but their perihelion distances are less than Earth's aphelion distance. They are smaller than 10 kilometers in size, and have relatively short lifetimes of about 10 million years.

Origin

Most Apollo asteroids are thought to have originated in the main asteroid belt, and were ejected into their current orbits by gravitational interactions with Jupiter.

Number

There are currently over 1,600 known Apollo asteroids.

Other names

Apollo asteroids are also known as Venus-crossers and Mars-crossers because their orbits cross those of these planets as well.
