Asteroids And Comets

Asteroids are rocky and metallic bodies mainly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, while comets are icy objects that originate from the Kuiper Belt or Oort Cloud. Comets develop glowing comas and tails when they near the Sun due to sublimation, whereas asteroids remain stable and don’t exhibit such features. Their differences in composition and orbits highlight their unique roles in the solar system.

Near-Earth Objects

Space is the vast, seemingly infinite expanse that exists beyond Earth’s atmosphere, encompassing all celestial bodies such as stars, planets, moons, and galaxies. Characterized by a near-perfect vacuum, it has extremely low pressure and density, allowing objects to move without atmospheric resistance. Within this cosmic realm, gravity governs the formation and movement of celestial bodies, while the interplay of time and space shapes our understanding of the universe.
