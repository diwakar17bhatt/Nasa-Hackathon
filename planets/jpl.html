Jet Propulsion Laboratory


About JPL


The Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) is a leading research and development center in Pasadena, California, focused on space exploration. Established in the 1930s, it originally began as a collaboration between scientists and engineers at Caltech and the U.S. government. JPL is best known for its robotic missions to planets and moons, including the Mars rovers, Voyager spacecraft, and the Galileo orbiter.


JPL operates under NASA and plays a key role in various space missions, emphasizing innovation in engineering and technology. Its projects not only advance our understanding of the solar system but also contribute to Earth science, climate monitoring, and astronomical research. The lab is recognized for its pioneering work in autonomous systems, propulsion, and mission design, making significant contributions to both scientific knowledge and technological advancements.
