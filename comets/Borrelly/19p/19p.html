Borrelly Comet


Discovery

Comet 19P/Borrelly was discovered by Alphonse Louis Nicolas Borrelly on Dec. 28, 1904, in Marseille, France.

Size

The nucleus of comet 19P/Borrelly is approximately 5 miles (8 kilometers) long and has dimensions of 8 × 4 × 4 kilometers. This makes it larger than 99% of asteroids, and comparable in size to the San Francisco Bay.

Distance from Earth

The distance of Comet 19P/Borrelly from Earth is currently 963,399,823.0 kilometers, equivalent to 6.4399300501 Astronomical Units. Light takes 53m 33.556s to travel from Comet 19P/Borrelly and arrive to us.

Velocity of Asteroid

The average orbit speed of Comet 19P/Borrelly is 15.70 kilometers per second (km/s). The comet's relative speed when the Deep Space 1 spacecraft flew by it in 2001 was 16.5 km/s.
